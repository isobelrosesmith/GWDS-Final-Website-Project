Typography Tasks

Axial

For this design I divided my text with a diagonal line, i placed more text on the bottom half to balance out the weight of the black section. I chose to use the colour red as it is bold and stands out on both the white and black.

Bilateral

Bilateral typography demonstrates symmetry and centered text, therefore i split my image in the middle and chose to alternate the colour of the text on either side to show contrast.

Dilatational

In Dilatational typography text is placed along circular paths forming curves. I typed along the edge of circles for accuracy and chose black as the background for dramatic effect.

Grid

In this grid design the text fits neatly into collums making it easy for the viewer to read with little distraction. I changed some font sizes to show hierachy within this outcome.

Modular

Repeated strucures such as my use of circles and boxes break up the text within modular typography. I also alternated the text colours to emphasise this.

Radial

This outcome is my favourite as the text flows out from one main focal point, i chose for this point to be central as it makes the text easier to read and is aesthetically pleasing.

Transitional

Transitional typography plays around with disorganisation, the text can either line up or go in different directions. I placed the longer piece of text in the middle to make it easier to read.

Random

Finally, for my random typography i experimented with the use of curved text, shapes and lines to break up the piece. I found this design the most fun to create as there are no boundries or rules.
